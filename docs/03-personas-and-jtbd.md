# Personas and Jobs-to-be-Done — FinOps Intelligence AI

## Primary Personas

### 1. Sarah — Chief Financial Officer

**Role:** CFO at a Series C SaaS company, $80M ARR, 320 employees.

**Responsibilities:** Board reporting, financial planning, budget governance, investor relations, finance operations oversight.

**Pain Points:**
1. Finds out about budget overruns and vendor surprises at month-end, not when they happen
2. Spends too much CFO time on operational variance investigation instead of strategic finance
3. Board presentations require emergency report compilation when surprises arise

**Jobs to be Done:**
1. Start every week knowing the top 5 financial risks without reading through spreadsheets
2. Approve AI-recommended actions in minutes, not hours, to stay on top of operational finance
3. Walk into board meetings with data-backed confidence, not anxiety about undiscovered issues

**Product Value:** Morning CFO brief delivers the week's risk summary, recommendations, and approval queue in a 2-minute review session. Sarah goes from reactive to proactive.

---

### 2. Priya — Finance Operations Manager

**Role:** Finance Ops Manager at a $45M ARR SaaS company, reports to VP Finance.

**Responsibilities:** Budget monitoring, vendor management, financial reporting, anomaly investigation, cross-functional coordination.

**Pain Points:**
1. Spends 60% of her time on reactive variance investigation instead of forward-looking analysis
2. Vendor renewals frequently catch her by surprise — no centralized tracking system
3. Manually compiling CFO reports takes a full day of work that could be automated

**Jobs to be Done:**
1. Know about spend anomalies and budget risks within hours, not weeks
2. Have vendor renewal calendar with usage data and negotiation recommendations ready at all times
3. Generate a CFO brief from live data in one click, not a day of manual work

**Product Value:** Priya's job transforms from report compiler to insight reviewer. She triages AI-generated findings, approves actions, and focuses on the 20% of issues that require human judgment.

---

### 3. Marcus — Engineering Operations Manager

**Role:** Eng Ops at a $60M ARR SaaS company. Manages cloud infrastructure spend and engineering productivity.

**Responsibilities:** Cloud cost optimization, tooling budget, contractor management, infrastructure planning.

**Pain Points:**
1. No visibility into which customer workloads are driving cloud cost spikes
2. Cloud bills are reactive — finds out about overruns in the monthly AWS invoice
3. No direct connection between cloud spend and gross margin impact on accounts

**Jobs to be Done:**
1. Receive specific workload-level recommendations when cloud costs spike, not just alerts
2. Know before the billing cycle which accounts are consuming disproportionate infrastructure
3. Act on optimization recommendations with enough lead time to make a difference

**Product Value:** Marcus receives specific Jira tasks with workload-level detail when the AI detects a cloud cost anomaly. He knows which customer accounts are driving the spike and has an estimated impact figure before he starts the investigation.

---

### 4. David — FP&A Lead

**Role:** FP&A Lead at a $120M ARR SaaS company. Owns financial planning, forecasting, and business partnering.

**Responsibilities:** Annual planning, quarterly reforecasting, budget variance analysis, board model maintenance.

**Pain Points:**
1. Quarterly reforecast requires days of manual data gathering and model updating
2. Budget variance explanations require detective work across multiple systems
3. Department heads get budget surprises at month-end because nobody warned them earlier

**Jobs to be Done:**
1. Have continuously updated budget forecasts available without manual model maintenance
2. Know the root cause of every material variance before the finance review meeting
3. Give department budget owners early warning with specific guidance, not surprise month-end emails

**Product Value:** David's planning models stay current automatically. Department heads receive weekly budget status summaries. Variance explanations are pre-computed with AI root cause analysis.

---

### 5. Alex — RevOps Manager

**Role:** RevOps Manager at a $35M ARR SaaS company. Manages CRM, sales tools, and revenue analytics.

**Responsibilities:** SaaS tool stack management, revenue analytics, CRM administration, license management.

**Pain Points:**
1. Multiple departments independently bought overlapping analytics tools — nobody tracks consolidation opportunities
2. Salesforce license utilization dropped to 71% but procurement doesn't know until renewal time
3. No visibility into which tools are genuinely being used vs. licensed-but-abandoned

**Jobs to be Done:**
1. Identify duplicate tool purchases across departments before they accumulate for years
2. Have usage health scores for every SaaS tool 60 days before renewal for maximum negotiation leverage
3. Build a case for SaaS consolidation with financial impact data, not just anecdotal evidence

**Product Value:** The Spend Anomaly Agent flags duplicate SaaS tools automatically. The Vendor Optimization Agent tracks license utilization and surfaces consolidation opportunities with estimated annual savings.

- **Controller** — Audit trail, compliance, actuals reconciliation
- **Board Member** — Quarterly financial health summary, risk exposure overview
- **CTO** — Cloud cost attribution, engineering efficiency metrics
