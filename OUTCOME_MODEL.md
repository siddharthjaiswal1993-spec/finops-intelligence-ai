# Outcome Model — FinOps Intelligence AI

How success is measured: what business outcomes the platform creates, what metrics track those outcomes, and what targets indicate the platform is working.

---

## The Value Hypothesis

FinOps Intelligence AI creates value by detecting financial risks earlier and by reducing the time from detection to approved action. Every week a budget overrun, vendor pricing change, or margin compression issue goes undetected represents real money that could have been saved or addressed.

The hypothesis: if AI agents can surface financial risks 3–6 weeks earlier than manual processes — and reduce the time from detection to approved corrective action from days to hours — then finance teams will avoid material financial losses that currently arrive as surprises.

Value is measured in dollars avoided and dollars saved, not productivity metrics.

---

## Business Impact Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Total risk identified per customer | >$500K per quarter | The platform has to justify its cost with clear financial upside |
| Savings realised (cost reduction) | >$200K per quarter | Identified savings only matter if they are acted on |
| Savings realisation rate | >70% of identified → realised | A high gap indicates the approval workflow is failing |
| Days earlier risk detected | >21 days vs. manual baseline | The core time-value proposition |
| CFO brief open rate | >90% weekly | If the CFO opens the brief every week, it has become a habit |

---

## Product Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Recommendation approval rate | >60% | Accepted recommendations are useful; rejected ones are noise |
| Anomaly true positive rate | >75% | Too many false positives and finance teams stop looking |
| Budget forecast accuracy | <10% error at 2-week horizon | The forecast has to be trustworthy before recommendations are |
| Time to first action from alert | <30 minutes | If the workflow is slow, the alert loses its value |
| Insight completeness rate | 100% (all 10 fields populated) | Partial insights do not drive action |

---

## AI Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Confidence calibration | Actual outcomes match stated confidence bands | An 80% confident insight should be correct 80% of the time |
| Cross-agent reasoning accuracy | Validated on 30-scenario golden set | Multi-source insights are the core differentiator |
| False positive rate | <25% | Acceptable threshold for finance context; below 15% is excellent |
| Root cause accuracy | >70% match to human expert assessment | Root cause wrong → wrong recommendation |

---

## Operational Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Data freshness | <4 hours for transactional data | Stale financial data produces wrong forecasts |
| Agent task completion rate | >99% daily | Missed agent runs mean missed signals |
| Approval workflow SLA | >90% resolved within 4 business hours | Finance decisions can have same-day deadlines |
| System uptime | >99.9% | CFO brief delivery on Monday morning has to be reliable |

---

## Customer Impact Metrics (Quarterly Business Reviews)

| Metric | What to Measure |
|---|---|
| Total risk surfaced | Dollar value of identified risks vs. baseline |
| Realised savings | Cost reductions that can be attributed to platform-driven actions |
| Analyst time saved | Hours per week freed from manual report building (target: >10 hours/analyst) |
| Risk-to-action cycle time | Days from signal to approved action vs. baseline |
| NPS | Finance ops team satisfaction score (target: >50) |

---

## The Metric That Matters Most

**CFO brief open rate.**

If the CFO opens the Monday brief every week without being reminded, the platform has earned a habit. Habits do not churn. The CFO brief is the first screen the platform needs to win — the downstream approval workflows, the agent activity, and the outcome tracking all follow from that first touch.

A platform that the CFO relies on weekly is a platform that will renew, expand, and generate internal champions. A platform the CFO ignores — no matter how good the AI is — will not survive its first renewal.
